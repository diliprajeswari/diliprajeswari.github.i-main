I’m the co-founder & CTO of LightHearted AI. We aim to prevent 10 million heart-related deaths by 2030.

At LightHearted, we have developed a laser-based stethoscope that uses light to acquire never-before-captured digital biomarkers of the heart sounds of the patient from several meters distance. This enables us to cheaply, non-invasively, and accurately diagnose cardiovascular conditions.

We have already run 2 pilot studies, and have published a patent and several articles. We are currently running a clinical trial at Queen Elizabeth University Hospital in Glasgow using a £1M grant that was won for the project

Precision Medicine has captivated my imagination since I was 15. We have dynamic personalised movie recommendations for entertainment, but when it comes to Healthcare, it’s mostly been passive with treatments based on trial & error. I have dedicated over five years to developing clinical-grade precision medicine platforms - from neurosurgery tools to personalised treatment recommendation solutions - demonstrating my commitment to advancing medical technology.

1 in 3 will die of cardiovascular disease, with the illness costing the global healthcare systems over $100B annually. In the UK alone, the urgency of the situation is highlighted by the 100,000 annual emergency admissions in life-or-death scenarios, despite evidence that community-level diagnostics could prevent premature deaths and improve quality of life.

Our innovative solution enables early and remote detection of heart disease patients, facilitating timely intervention through pharmaceutical treatments and lifestyle modifications. Our device is designed with the vision of seamless integration into multifaceted health monitoring systems, enhancing its utility in both clinical and home settings, further enabling Precision Medicine and Preventative Healthcare for a globally ageing population.
